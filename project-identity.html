Identity I & II Project

I designed the above poster in response to another student's identity booklet that questioned how advertising and subliminal messages may influence people's identities.

The Cape Verdeans' arrival in the United States during the late 19th century led to a shift in their greater identity. A people of mixed ancestry, Cape Verdeans who identified as Portuguese and white on the islands found themselves forced into a Black American identity that ignored and denied their heritage.

Using anecdotes in Marilyn Halter's Between race and ethnicity: Cape Verdean American Immigrants, 1860-1965, family photos, and public documents, I designed a french-fold, stab-stitch bound booklet expressing this disillusionment with an unwanted identity. In reference to the quote "Theirs is an ethnic tapestry shot through with threads..." I wove thread throughout the pages to altar or emphasize the original narratives.
